Professional Background

I’m Isabela, an Event Management major at the University of Central Florida with a strong focus on planning, logistics, and operations. I enjoy figuring out how all the moving parts of an event can fit together to create a seamless and memorable experience. I thrive in environments where I can bring structure to complexity—aligning people, resources, and timing to make big ideas feel effortless.

My professional background includes several guest-facing and leadership roles at Walt Disney World, as well as serving as the Operations & Exhibition Services Lead for UCF’s Rosen College Career Fair. In that role, I managed logistical operations for a 500+ attendee event, coordinating floor plans, signage, vendor load-in/load-out, and risk management. I also collaborated cross-functionally to oversee registration, parking, guest experience, and on-site problem-solving. Across all of my roles, I’ve become known as someone who stays calm under pressure and steps in as a dependable team lead when it matters most.

I’m especially proud of maintaining strong academic performance—earning Dean’s and President’s List honors—while working full-time. This balance reflects how seriously I take my commitments and how motivated I am to grow. I approach every project with empathy, organization, and initiative, and I’m always looking for new ways to contribute meaningfully while supporting those around me.

A Little About Me

When I’m not working, I enjoy slow moments—movies, theme park strolls, books, and quality time with my family. I’m also passionate about animal rescue—specifically fostering abandoned kittens. What started as a spontaneous act of care quickly became a personal mission I’ve embraced wholeheartedly.

Rescuing vulnerable animals has taught me patience, perspective, and the importance of showing up when it matters most. Whether I’m caring for a newborn kitten or supporting a team during a fast-paced event, I bring that same empathy, energy, and follow-through to everything I do.
